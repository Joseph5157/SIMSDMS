SIMS College of Pharmacy · Discipline Duty

Your duty, on your phone.

A short, hands-on guide to the Discipline Management System (DMS) — how to check in for duty, pick your slots, log a student violation, and swap a duty when you can't make it. No paper registers, no chasing the office.

📲 Works on your phone ✈️ Sign in with Telegram ⏱️ 5 core tasks

In this session

Why we're moving to DMS → the 5 things you'll actually do → a one-page cheat sheet to keep.

The why

Why we're using DMS

Discipline duty used to live in paper registers and WhatsApp messages. Attendance was hard to prove, slot swaps got lost, and student fines were tracked in a notebook. DMS puts all of it in one place — accurate, timestamped, and visible to everyone who needs it.

📋

One record of duty

Your check-in and check-out are timestamped automatically. No signing a register, no disputes about who was present.

🔄

Swaps that don't get lost

Can't make a duty? Send it to a colleague in the app. They accept, and the duty officially moves — with a trail.

⚠️

Student violations, logged fairly

Record a violation with the exact fine set by admin. The student and office see the same record you do.

✈️

Reminders find you

Telegram tells you when duty is coming up, when a swap needs your answer, and when you forgot to clock out.

Before

  • Paper register at the gate
  • Swaps arranged over calls

Getting started

Signing in & finding your way

You sign in once with a one-time code sent to Telegram — no password to remember. After that, everything lives on five tabs along the bottom of the screen.

  1. Open the DMS link your admin shared and enter your registered email.

  2. A one-time code (OTP) arrives in Telegram. Type it in to sign in.

  3. You land on your Dashboard — today's duty, quick actions, and recent activity.

  4. Move between areas using the bottom tabs. That's the whole app.

💡 First time? If you haven't linked Telegram yet, open the link your admin sent and tap Start in the bot — then the codes will reach you.

Task 01 · Every duty day

Check in when you arrive, out when you leave

This is the one thing you do on every duty day. It replaces the paper register — your times are saved the moment you tap.

  1. Open the Dashboard. Your duty for today shows at the top as a blue “Today's duty” card.

  2. When you arrive, tap Check In. The card shows your check-in time.

  3. When you leave, tap Check Out on the same card.

  4. Have both a morning and afternoon duty? Each session has its own card — check in and out separately.

⏰ Forgot to clock out? The system auto clocks-out at session end, but that shows as “Auto”. You'll get a reminder 15 minutes before — clock out yourself to keep it clean.

Task 02 · Start of the month

Pick your duty slots for the month

When the admin opens the scheduling window, you choose which days you'll be on duty. You pick a set number of sessions (usually three).

  1. Go to the Slots tab. A green banner shows the window is open and how many you have left to pick.

  2. Tap any highlighted date on the calendar (blue = a session is available).

  3. In the panel, tap Pick Morning or Pick Afternoon.

  4. Picked days turn green. Repeat until you've hit your required count.

💡 Changed your mind? Once picked, ask your admin to reassign it, or send a reassignment request to a colleague (Task 03). You can't un-pick past your limit.

Task 03 · During duty

Record a student violation

Only possible while you're on an active duty session — so check in first. The fine is set automatically from the admin's list; you don't decide the amount.

  1. On the Dashboard (during duty) tap Record Student Violation.

  2. Search the student by name or registration number and select them.

  3. Choose the violation type — the fine fills in on its own. Tick Warning only if no fine applies.

  4. Add optional notes, then tap Record Student Violation.

💡 Several at once? Turn on Quick-add mode — the form stays open and clears after each save so you can log the next student fast.

Task 04 · When you can't make it

Ask a colleague to cover your duty

Can't attend an upcoming duty? Send it to a colleague. The duty only moves once they accept — until then it's still yours.

  1. On the Dashboard, find the duty under Upcoming duties and tap Request reassignment.

  2. Pick a colleague from the eligible list and add an optional reason.

  3. Tap Send Request. The row now shows “requested — pending”.

  4. Changed your mind while it's still pending? Tap Cancel request on the same row.

🔁 Not confirmed until accepted. If your colleague declines, the duty stays with you — you'll see “declined” on the row. Send it to someone else or speak to your admin.

Task 05 · When a colleague asks you

Accept or decline a duty someone sends you

When a colleague asks you to cover, it appears on your Dashboard and pings you on Telegram. Your answer is what makes the swap official.

  1. Open the Dashboard. Incoming requests sit near the top under “Reassignment requests”.

  2. Check the date, session, and who's asking (and their reason).

  3. Tap Accept to take the duty — it's now yours and shows on your calendar.

  4. Or tap Reject if you can't. The duty stays with the requester.

✈️ You can also tap Accept / Reject straight from the Telegram message — no need to open the app.

Good to know

Check your record & fix a mistake

Two tabs let you look back at what you've done and correct honest mistakes.

  1. Attendance tab → your monthly summary: on-time, late, and auto clock-outs, split by morning/afternoon, with full history.

  2. Violations tab → every student violation you've recorded, with student, type, fine, and status.

  3. Logged the wrong thing? Tap Delete on a violation to remove it (you'll confirm first).

  4. Unsure about a record? Tap Flag to send it to admin for review instead of deleting.

🗑️ Delete is permanent and can't be undone from the app. If you're not sure, Flag it instead and let admin decide.

Staying on top of it

Telegram keeps you in the loop

You don't have to keep opening the app to stay current. The DMS bot messages you on Telegram for the things that need you.

  1. Duty reminders — a heads-up before your session so you don't miss a check-in.

  2. Swap requests — when a colleague asks you to cover, with Accept / Reject buttons right in the chat.

  3. Clock-out reminder — a nudge near the end of your session.

  4. Type /menu to the bot any time for quick shortcuts.

🆘 Stuck? Locked out, no OTP arriving, or a wrong record you can't fix — contact your Admin. They can reset your login and reassign duties.

SIMS DMS — Faculty Quick Reference

Keep this handy. Everything you need for discipline duty, in one page.

🔑 Sign in

  1. Open the DMS link, enter your email.
  2. Get the OTP on Telegram, type it in.
  3. Land on your Dashboard. No password needed.

📋 Check in / out

  1. Dashboard → Today's duty card.
  2. Arrive → Check In.
  3. Leave → Check Out.
  4. Two duties = two cards, done separately.

📅 Pick slots

  1. Slots tab (when window is open).
  2. Tap a blue highlighted date.
  3. Pick Morning / Pick Afternoon.
  4. Green = picked. Hit your required count.

⚠️ Record violation

  1. Must be checked in / on duty.
  2. Dashboard → Record Student Violation.
  3. Search student → pick type (fine auto-fills).
  4. Record. Use Quick-add for many.

🔄 Swap a duty

  1. Upcoming duty → Request reassignment.
  2. Pick a colleague → Send Request.
  3. Moves only when they Accept.
  4. Still pending? Cancel request.

✅ Respond & review

  1. Asked to cover? Dashboard → Accept / Reject.
  2. Attendance tab = your history.
  3. Wrong record? Flag for review, or Delete.

Remember

• Record violations only while on duty. • Fines are set by admin — you don't choose the amount. • Delete is permanent; when unsure, Flag instead. • Forgot to check out? You'll be auto clocked-out, shown as “Auto”.

Need help?

No OTP, locked out, or a record you can't fix → contact your Admin. Reminders come via the Telegram bot — type /menu for shortcuts.
